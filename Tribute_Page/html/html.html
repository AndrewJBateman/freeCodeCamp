Pau Casals

One of the greatest cellists of the 20th century

-Pau Casals reading music at his desk

Here is a time line of Pau (also known as Pablo) Casals' life:

  • 1876 - born in El Vendrell, Tarragona, Spain.
  • 1882 - first musical performance in the nearby church.
  • 1888 - Pau sees and hears a cello for the first time.
  • 1891 - Meets the composer Enrique Granados and they become friends.
  • 1895 - Moves to Paris but is not happy and moves back to Barcelona.
  • 1899 - Moves to Paris again and lives in a house with an American singer Emma Nevada and her husband. He then travels to London with her and another musician. They give a private performance to the Queen at her house on the Isle of Wight.
  • 1904 - Travels to the US again as a soloist. First concert at Carnegie Hall.
  • 1906 - The trio Cortot-Thibaud-Casals debut in Lille, France. They become the most important musical trio of their time.
  • 1912 - Interprests a Brahms double concert with Eugène Ysaÿe in the Gran Salón of the Musikverein in Viena.
  • 1915 - Makes the first recordings for the Columbia Gramaphone Company.
  • 1920 - Founds, with Alfred Cortot and Jacques Thibaud, the École Normale de Musique in París, where he gives courses in interpretation every summer.
  • 1926 - Creates the Concert Workers Association, an association of independant characters witht he ultimate intention of bringing music to the working classes.
  • 1928 - Realizes his last concert as a soloist in the US, in the New York Town Hall, with Nicolai Mednikoff at the piano.
  • 1933 - Offers his last concerts with Jacques Thibaud and Alfred Cortot, finalizing a colaboration that has lasted 26 years.
  • 1936 - The Pau Casals Orchestra completes its last season.
  • 1940 - In june the Germans enter Paris. Pau Casals, together with Francesca Capdevila, Pilar Casals and the Alavedra family, leave France for North America.
  • 1940 - In june the Germans enter Paris. Pau Casals, together with Francesca Capdevila, Pilar Casals and the Alavedra family, leave France for North America.
  • 1945 - End of the Second World War. Gives a concert at the Royal Albert House in London with the BBC Symphony Orchestra conducted by Sir Adrian Boult.
  • 1950 - Presides over the Floral Games of the Catalan Language in exile, celebrated in Perpinan.
  • 1955 - Travels extensively in Puerto Rico and inaugorates the Casals Festival the following year.
  • 1960 - Gives a series of masterclasses in the Music Department at the University of Berkeley.
  • 1965 - Conducts The Pessebre in the Carnegie Institute of Technology in Pittsburgh, US, and the Brandenburgh Concertos of Johann Sebastian Bach.
  • 1971 - Receives the Grand Order of the Cross from France. Composes the Hym of the United Nations.
  • 1973 - Goes to Jerusalem to conduct the Youth Orchestra and the Jerusalem Symphony Orchestra.
  • 1973 - Dies in Puerto Rico.
